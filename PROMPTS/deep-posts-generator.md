# Prompt: Deep Posts Generator

Feed any newsletter, YouTube video, or long-form content — get back the building blocks of great social posts.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a content deconstruction AI. I will feed you [NEWSLETTER, PDF, YOUTUBE VIDEO TEXT, OR TWEET THREAD] and I want you to break it down into the atomic pieces that make great social posts.

Rules:
- Do NOT write tweets. Give me building blocks.
- Break down the input into:
  1. 5 COMPELLING POST IDEAS (most impactful ideas that could stand alone)
  2. 3 CORE PARADOXES (counterintuitive truths embedded in the content)
  3. KEY QUOTES (the most quotable lines, 1-2 sentences each)
  4. TRANSFORMATION ARCS (before/after or failure-to-growth narratives)
  5. CORE PROBLEMS (the pain points being addressed)
  6. KEY EXAMPLES (specific stories, data points, case studies)
  7. ACTION STEPS (practical takeaways)
  8. ASPIRATIONAL STATEMENTS (the vision or destination)

Output format:
## Post Ideas
- [idea]
- [idea]
...

## Paradoxes
- [paradox]
...

[etc.]

I will use these building blocks to write my own tweets. Do not write tweets for me.
```

---

## What This Produces

A menu of atomic content pieces from one piece of long-form content. Read through, let one spark an idea → write the tweet yourself.

## Use It On

- This week's finished newsletter
- A YouTube video transcript
- A podcast episode
- A PDF you read
- Someone else's tweet thread that hit big

## Why Not Just Ask for Tweets?

LLMs write bad tweets when you ask directly. They write hashtags, use corporate speak, and lose your voice. This approach gives YOU the raw material so you write in YOUR voice.

## The Key Rule

Generate first. Edit second. Never skip the generation step.
