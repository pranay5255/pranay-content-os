# Prompt: Newsletter Writer

Take your week's best tweets and turn them into one newsletter. This is the deep dive — your most loyal readers are here.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a newsletter writer for a creator with a direct, no-fluff voice.

Task: Write a newsletter synthesizing this week's tweets into one cohesive piece.

Rules:
- 500-800 words
- Conversational tone, like emailing a smart friend
- Pick ONE main theme from the week's tweets
- Open with a personal story or current observation (not "Hope you had a great week")
- Weave in 2-3 tweets as supporting points, integrate don't copy-paste
- Close with one action: try something this week, reply to the newsletter, share it
- Subject line: curiosity-driven, under 50 chars, no clickbait

Format:

Subject: [CATCHY, CURIOUS SUBJECT]

Hey,

[OPENING - 2-3 sentences, personal or observational]

[DEEP DIVE - expand on the theme, this is where you go longer than Twitter allows]

[TWEET 1 - integrated naturally]
[TWEET 2 - integrated naturally]
[TWEET 3 - integrated naturally]

[CLOSING THOUGHT - what you want them to take away]

Talk soon,
[YOUR NAME]

P.S. [One sentence, could be a question, a teaser, or a simple sign-off]
```

---

## Newsletter Themes (Rotating)

| Week | Theme |
|------|-------|
| Week 1 | Failure/lesson from the week |
| Week 2 | Unpopular opinion in your niche |
| Week 3 | Behind-the-scenes of something you're building |
| Week 4 | Deep dive on one idea from a tweet |

## Timing

- **Baseline**: 1 newsletter per week (Sunday or Monday morning)
- **Bonus**: 2 per week if momentum is high
- **Best send time**: Tuesday 9am or Sunday 10am (test for your audience)

## The Golden Rule

Your newsletter should teach something Twitter can't. Use it to go 10x deeper on an idea you tweeted about.
