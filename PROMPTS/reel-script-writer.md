# Prompt: Reel Script Writer

Turn your tweet into a 60-second reel/TikTok script. The tweet is your outline — you add 20% on camera.

---

## The Prompt

```
You are a short-form video scriptwriter.

Task: Turn this tweet into a 60-second reel script.

Rules:
- 60 seconds = ~150 words (natural pacing)
- Hook in first 3 seconds (say the controversial/curious part first)
- Body: 3-5 points, one per sentence, conversational
- CTA at end: "Follow for more" or ask a question
- Write how you'd actually talk, not how you'd write
- No jargon, no buzzwords

Format:
[HOOK - 3 seconds, say this first]
[Then say this]
[Then this]
[Then this]
[End with this]

---

TWEET:
[TURN THIS INTO A REEL SCRIPT]
```

---

## Reel Script Anatomy

```
0-3s:   HOOK — the controversial/uncomfortable claim
3-20s:  SETUP — who this is for, why you care
20-40s: UNPACK — the insight, with one concrete example
40-55s: CONTRAST — before/after, or what most people get wrong
55-60s: CTA + hook for next video
```

## How to Film (Dan Koe Style)

1. Open camera, flip to front-facing
2. Read your tweet (memorize it first or put it on screen)
3. Add 20% — expand on the emotion, the story, the "why"
4. Don't do multiple takes — imperfection is authentic
5. Post immediately, don't overthink

## The 60-Second Rule

- If you can't say it in 60 seconds, it's too complex
- Cut everything that isn't essential
- Pause = dead air = lost viewers

## Batch Filming

Film 5 reels in one sitting every 2 weeks. That's 10 reels = 2.5 weeks of content.
