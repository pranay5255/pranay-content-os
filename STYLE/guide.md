## Do This

- Start with the hook, not the setup
- End tweets with a question or pattern interrupt
- Use numbers ("3 things", "I failed 3 times")
- Be specific ("I lost $12k" not "I lost money")
- Say "I don't know" when you don't
- Admit when you changed your mind

---

## Don't Do This

- Generic advice ("Work hard, stay consistent")
- Threads that should be tweets (if you can't fit it in 280, you probably can)
- Hashtags (except on LinkedIn if you must)
- Threads for things that don't need 5 slides
- Fake energy or hype language

---

## Topics to Tweet About

### High Engagement
- What you changed your mind about recently
- Industry insider observations
- Failure stories with specific details
- Contrarian takes on popular advice

### Lower Priority (but useful for variety)
- Book/podcast takeaways (add your own angle)
- Behind-the-scenes of what you're building
- Questions you don't know the answer to (engages the audience)

---

## Avoid

- Crypto / get rich quick framing
- Long threads when a tweet suffices
- Posting purely promotional content
- Copying others' angles (even Dan Koe's — use as inspiration, not template)

---

## Formatting Rules

| Platform | Format |
|----------|--------|
| Tweet | Plain text, 280 chars, no emoji |
| Thread | Numbered slides, 200 chars/slide |
| LinkedIn | Story format, paragraphs, no emoji |
| Reel | Scripted but natural, 60s |
| Newsletter | Long-form, personal, action-driven |
